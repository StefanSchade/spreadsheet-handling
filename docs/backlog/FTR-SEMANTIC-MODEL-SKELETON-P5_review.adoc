== Structural Assessment

The 18 top-level chapters in `semantic_model.adoc` match the seed list in
the FTR exactly and in order:

. Management Summary
. Relation To Other Models
. Problem Statement
. Ambiguity Policy
. External Inspirations
. Project Semantic Model
. Overlapping Semantic Sets
. Lifecycle And Update Semantics
. Registry Role
. Roundtrip Walkthroughs
. Conflict And Precedence Questions
. Contract Matrices
. Semantic Guards
. Versioning And Evolution
. Adoption Strategy
. Planned FTRs
. Open Questions
. Glossary

Each chapter now carries a stable `[[semantic-model-<slug>]]` anchor
immediately preceding the `==` heading, which gives per-section FTR work a
durable cross-reference target. Anchor slugs are derived literally from the
chapter titles, so future title polish must be deliberate (acceptable
trade-off, and consistent with the FTR's intent to lock the layout).

The promotion of "Ambiguity Policy" to a top-level chapter is structurally
sound:

* Its previous parent ("Problem Statement", subsection "Why this matters more
  here than in ordinary CRUD systems") now ends naturally on the deterministic
  roundtrip paragraph, which reads as a closing argument for the Problem
  Statement rather than a setup for ambiguity rules.
* The Ambiguity Policy content states general project-wide policy ("Not every
  ambiguity is a bug ...") that genuinely belongs above the Problem Statement
  level; the promotion does not distort intent and removes an implicit
  ambiguity about where the policy applies.

The "Glossary Draft" -> "Glossary" rename is harmless: there are no inbound
references to the old title in the repo, and the document body already
contains the "draft scaffold" marker at the top, so removing the redundant
"Draft" qualifier from the chapter title does not undermine the draft status.

The draft-scaffold marker is preserved and lightly reinforced:

----
This document is a draft scaffold.
[...]
The top-level chapters are the stabilized scaffold for later per-section FTRs.
----

The added sentence is a structural statement ("the scaffold is now stable"),
not a semantic commitment, and does not constitute hardening.

The decision to keep the document single-file at this phase remains
reasonable: chapter granularity is now addressable via anchors, no chapter is
yet large enough to justify the include overhead, and the FTR explicitly
allows the single-file choice. Per-section FTRs can split chapters later
without coordination cost because the anchors are already in place.

== Residual Risks

* The chapter-anchor slugs are derived literally from the current chapter
  titles. If a later per-section FTR retitles a chapter, the anchor will
  become a name/title mismatch unless renamed in lockstep. Acceptable for
  now because the FTR explicitly locks the chapter list; flagged so the
  per-section FTRs know to treat anchor renames as deliberate.
* The semantic model is still excluded from the public Pages bundle. This is
  intentional per the FTR, but downstream FTRs should keep this in mind to
  avoid accidentally promoting a draft scaffold to a public surface.
* `docs/backlog/phase_review_semantic_model_foundations.adoc` references the
  historical path `docs/semantic_model.adoc/semantic_model_seed.adoc`. The
  current state matches the migration described there, so the reference
  remains historically accurate and is not stale.
