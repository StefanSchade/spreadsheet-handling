== Executive summary

`FTR-DYNAMIC-FRAME-PROJECTION-P5` was written when the column-role taxonomy
(`row_identity`, `display_helper`, `matrix_value`) was implicit. With the
foundation FTR now naming those roles, the projection FTR is no longer
underdetermined but it still carries hidden assumptions that the foundation
now owns: it reconstructs role semantics inline in *Context*, frames its
*Examples* in column-name terms instead of role terms, and is silent on the
inbound projection mirror that the driver analysis (`review_dynamic_projection_analysis.adoc`,
§7) explicitly flagged.

The right disposition is *refine*, not *split* and not *supersede*. The
FTR's real concern -- *should* the framework offer declarative ordering and
retention of projected columns, and what does the surface look like? --
remains a single well-shaped question. The refinements are wording- and
scope-level: declare a dependency on the foundation, state that role
classification is consumed not redefined, add the inbound mirror to scope,
remove the FK-helper aliasing aside, and recast the examples in role terms.

No content in the FTR is invalidated. None of the *Architectural Options*
(A keep plugins / B add ergonomics / C reuse metadata) changes meaning;
option C in particular becomes the natural baseline once the foundation
ships, because "reuse metadata" is exactly what consuming the role
classification means.

== Current overlaps with the foundation FTR

This section lists every place in the projection FTR where the foundation
FTR already owns, or now better owns, the underlying concept.

The two FTRs do *not* overlap on:

* the projection *mechanism* (how the pipeline step actually reorders or
  trims columns at runtime),
* the declarative *surface* (YAML keys, ordering syntax, `*` semantics),
* the architectural *decision* (declarative vs. plugin),
* the *evaluation* of whether the feature is worth its surface-area cost.

Those remain the projection FTR's sole concern.

== Hidden assumptions now owned upstream

These are points where the projection FTR's text *presupposes* concepts
the foundation now defines. Left unrefined, they would either re-derive
the foundation's content or contradict it.

. *Helpers and keys exist as nameable column classes.* The projection FTR
  treats this as obvious. Foundation FTR makes it explicit and names the
  classes (`display_helper`, `row_identity`). Refinement should phrase
  projection in terms of *those names*, not "helper" and "key" as
  informal lowercase nouns.
. *The "remainder" set is the projection's value cells.* The projection
  FTR uses positional language ("all remaining columns"). Foundation FTR
  scopes that rest-by-exclusion *only within a projected matrix / view
  surface whose intended role partition is exactly the three roles*; it
  is not a global "everything else" rule. The projection FTR must not
  imply a global rest semantics.
. *Reimport restores the same column partition.* The projection FTR
  notes that the consumer plugin "reconstruct[s] the corresponding
  payload view during reimport", but does not state the requirement
  declaratively. Foundation FTR Scope, "minimal reimport-survival
  requirement", states that `row_identity` and `display_helper` must
  survive at least one outbound -> inbound roundtrip, and `matrix_value`
  is then recoverable by exclusion. The projection FTR should cite this
  rather than restate it.
. *Helpers always appear at the leading edge.* The projection FTR's
  examples (`title \| story_id \| <dynamic character columns>`) and the
  YAML sketches (`leading_columns: [title, story_id]`) phrase the role
  positionally. Foundation FTR *Future Design Pressure (Non-Scope)*
  explicitly states "Helper semantics are not row-only ... the role is
  positional-neutral". The projection FTR must not hardwire `display_helper`
  to a leading position; that is one rendering of the role, not a
  definition of it.
. *`contract_xref` is the sole authority for keys; `configure_workbook_view`
  for helpers.* The projection FTR's *Context* implies this. Foundation
  FTR's "metadata source / consumer expectations" softened this:
  multiple steps may contribute role metadata; no step is the monolithic
  owner. The projection FTR should not assume single-owner sourcing.

== Findings

Findings use the disposition pattern from `docs/ai_info/review_instructions.adoc`
§ "Findings and prioritization": observation, evidence, risk, recommended
action, priority.

=== R1 -- Declare dependency on the foundation FTR (priority: required for refinement)

* *Observation.* `FTR-DYNAMIC-FRAME-PROJECTION-P5` does not cite
  `FTR-PROJECTED-FRAME-COLUMN-SEMANTICS-P5` anywhere.
* *Evidence.* The FTR's *Context*, *Scope*, *Examples*, and
  *Architectural Options* sections all reconstruct role semantics
  inline. The driver review (`review_dynamic_projection_analysis.adoc`,
  §6 and §7) recommends recording the foundation as a dependency.
* *Risk.* Without an explicit dependency declaration, future readers
  may rederive the role taxonomy here or, worse, define a parallel one
  in `FTR-DYNAMIC-VIEW-COLUMN-TARGETING-P5` and discover the conflict
  only at implementation time.
* *Recommended action.* Add a top-level *Depends on:* line citing the
  foundation FTR; cross-link from *Context* and from Option C in
  *Architectural Options*.

=== R2 -- Recast scope and examples in role terms, not positional terms (priority: refinement)

* *Observation.* The FTR's *Context*, *Examples*, and the embedded YAML
  sketches frame projection positionally ("leading columns", "remaining
  columns", `leading_columns: [...]`, `keep_remaining: true`).
* *Evidence.* The foundation FTR's *Future Design Pressure (Non-Scope)*
  states helper semantics are positional-neutral. The driver review
  (§5) recommends "modelling first, ergonomics last".
* *Risk.* Positional framing leaks an unintentional constraint: it
  implies `display_helper` always renders at the leading edge, which
  forecloses the matrix-axis and header-structure cases that the
  foundation deliberately keeps open.
* *Recommended action.* Restate scope as "ordering and retention *by
  role*", with positional ordering presented as one possible *render*
  choice over the role classification, not as the definition. The
  existing YAML sketches can remain as illustrative examples if marked
  as one possible surface among several.

=== R3 -- Add the inbound projection mirror to scope (priority: refinement)

* *Observation.* `FTR-DYNAMIC-FRAME-PROJECTION-P5` is currently phrased
  in outbound terms (workbook-render-side). The inbound side
  (`build_story_cast_matrix_payload`, "reconstruct the corresponding
  payload view during reimport") is mentioned once in *Context* but is
  not a scope item.
* *Evidence.* Driver review §3 (workaround element
  `build_story_cast_matrix_payload`) and §7 ("`FTR-DYNAMIC-FRAME-PROJECTION-P5`
  should add the *inbound* projection mirror ... to its scope") flag
  this gap explicitly.
* *Risk.* If left outbound-only, the FTR can be "implemented" without
  removing the consumer-side inbound plugin, which is half the
  worldbuilding workaround. The asymmetry would persist.
* *Recommended action.* Add an explicit scope item for inbound
  projection: retain `row_identity ∪ matrix_value` by role, drop
  `display_helper`. Both directions share the same role classification,
  so the implementation cost of adding the mirror is small once the
  outbound side exists.

=== R4 -- Reduce the *Context* and *Scope* role-reconstruction prose (priority: scope reduction)

* *Observation.* *Context* lists the worldbuilding plugin's role-based
  behaviors inline (helpers first, keys fixed, append remainder,
  reconstruct payload); *Scope* asks the project to "Evaluate recurring
  dynamic projection patterns" and "Assess whether existing primitives
  are sufficient", which both depend on the role taxonomy.
* *Evidence.* Foundation FTR Scope (role definitions, reimport-survival
  rule, metadata-source expectations) now owns the role taxonomy
  end-to-end.
* *Risk.* Duplicate definition of the same concept in two FTRs creates
  drift; later edits to the foundation may leave the projection FTR
  silently inconsistent.
* *Recommended action.* Replace the inline role reconstruction with a
  one-sentence pointer ("Column roles `row_identity`, `display_helper`,
  `matrix_value` are defined in `FTR-PROJECTED-FRAME-COLUMN-SEMANTICS-P5`;
  this FTR consumes them.") and trim *Scope* items to the *mechanism*
  and *surface* questions.

=== R5 -- Relocate or trim the FK-helper aliasing aside (priority: scope reduction)

* *Observation.* The trailing *Additional Consumer Validation
  Observation* documents the `_regions_name` -> `region_name` FK-helper
  naming ergonomics observation.
* *Evidence.* Foundation FTR Non-Goals explicitly classifies this as a
  *projection ergonomics* question, "not a role-classification
  question", and points at this FTR by name.
* *Risk.* The aside currently lives in two places (this FTR and the
  foundation's Non-Goals). If the projection FTR is refined to focus
  on the role-classification consumption case, the aside becomes
  off-topic here too; leaving it in invites scope creep on review.
* *Recommended action.* Either trim the aside to a one-line pointer
  ("FK-helper name aliasing remains a separate ergonomics question
  outside the role-classification scope; see foundation FTR's
  Non-Goals") or relocate it to its own backlog entry. Do not perform
  the move as part of this alignment review.

=== R6 -- Update Option C framing in *Architectural Options* (priority: refinement)

* *Observation.* Option C is phrased as "Investigate whether ... metadata
  can provide sufficient information". With the foundation FTR, the
  *what* is no longer speculative; only the *how* (where the metadata
  is read from and how the step composes) remains.
* *Evidence.* Foundation FTR names the classification and the
  reimport-survival rule.
* *Risk.* If Option C is left as speculative investigation, the
  decision documented under *Acceptance Criteria* may revert to the
  pre-foundation framing and accidentally re-open the modelling
  question.
* *Recommended action.* Reframe Option C as "Consume the column-role
  classification from `FTR-PROJECTED-FRAME-COLUMN-SEMANTICS-P5` and
  decide whether the consumption surface lives on the existing
  workbook-view step, a new projection step, or both." Keep Options A
  and B unchanged.

=== R7 -- Add an explicit *Non-Goals* exclusion for role-taxonomy work (priority: refinement)

* *Observation.* The FTR's *Non-Goals* does not state that the role
  taxonomy itself is out of scope.
* *Evidence.* Foundation FTR owns the taxonomy; without an explicit
  exclusion in the projection FTR, future drafts could quietly expand
  scope.
* *Risk.* Scope creep on review; the foundation/projection boundary
  may erode.
* *Recommended action.* Add a *Non-Goals* item: "Defining or
  redefining the column-role taxonomy. The taxonomy
  (`row_identity`, `display_helper`, `matrix_value`) is owned by
  `FTR-PROJECTED-FRAME-COLUMN-SEMANTICS-P5`."

=== N1 -- Non-finding: Architectural Options A and B remain valid (priority: non-finding)

* *Observation.* Options A (keep plugins) and B (add projection
  ergonomics) are unchanged by the foundation FTR.
* *Evidence.* Both options are about *surface*, not about *role
  classification*. They are orthogonal to the foundation.
* *Risk.* None.
* *Recommended action.* Keep as written. They are the actual decision
  this FTR is responsible for and they survive the refactor untouched.

=== N2 -- Non-finding: FTR is not superseded (priority: non-finding)

* *Observation.* The projection FTR still owns the
  declarative-vs-plugin decision and the ergonomics question. Those
  are not in the foundation's scope and the foundation explicitly
  defers them.
* *Evidence.* Foundation FTR *Non-Goals*: "Dynamic projection
  implementation. How a step actually orders or trims columns by role
  is `FTR-DYNAMIC-FRAME-PROJECTION-P5`'s scope."
* *Risk.* None.
* *Recommended action.* Reject the *superseded* verdict.

== Recommended changes (summary)

The findings above translate to *seven small text-level edits* on
`FTR-DYNAMIC-FRAME-PROJECTION-P5.adoc`. No section is removed in full;
no new section beyond *Depends on:* and a *Non-Goals* item is added.

. Add a *Depends on:* line citing `FTR-PROJECTED-FRAME-COLUMN-SEMANTICS-P5`
  near the FTR header (R1).
. Replace the inline role reconstruction in *Context* with a one-sentence
  pointer to the foundation FTR's role definitions (R4).
. Recast *Scope* items in role terms ("ordering and retention by role")
  and drop scope items that re-evaluate role taxonomy (R2, R4).
. Add an inbound-projection scope item: "Apply the same role-based
  ordering and retention symmetrically on the reimport path; retain
  `row_identity ∪ matrix_value`, drop `display_helper`." (R3)
. Re-label *Examples* YAML sketches as "one possible surface over the
  role classification" rather than as the underlying concept; preserve
  the existing YAML for illustration (R2).
. Reframe *Architectural Options* Option C as "Consume the foundation
  FTR's classification; decide where the consumption surface lives"
  (R6).
. Add a *Non-Goals* item: "Defining or redefining the column-role
  taxonomy ..." citing the foundation FTR (R7).
. Trim the *Additional Consumer Validation Observation* (FK-helper
  aliasing aside) to a one-line pointer or remove it (R5).

Concrete edit text is *not* embedded here verbatim; the findings name
the precise targets and the foundation FTR provides the canonical
wording for the role classifications.

== Suggested scope reductions

The projection FTR can shrink along three axes without losing meaning:

* *Role-taxonomy reconstruction.* Approximately the first half of
  *Context* (the enumeration of helpers, keys, remainder, payload
  reconstruction) collapses to one sentence and a pointer to the
  foundation FTR. (R4)
* *Inline column-name examples promoted to surfaces.* The `title \|
  story_id \| <dynamic character columns>` example need not be
  reasoned about in the FTR's own prose; the worldbuilding case
  enters as a single illustrative example with the role mapping
  shown once. (R2)
* *FK-helper aliasing aside.* Either relocate or trim. (R5)

The projection FTR's *Scope*, *Non-Goals*, *Examples* (the YAML
sketches), *Architectural Options*, *Acceptance Criteria*,
*Validation*, *Discussion Note*, and *Motivation* sections all
survive in some form. None disappears.

== Remaining responsibilities of FTR-DYNAMIC-FRAME-PROJECTION-P5

After refinement, the projection FTR is responsible for exactly the
following, none of which is owned by the foundation:

* *The decision.* Should the framework offer declarative ordering and
  retention of projected columns, or should this remain a consumer-side
  plugin concern? (Options A, B, C.)
* *The mechanism.* If declarative, how does the pipeline step actually
  reorder or trim columns at runtime, and where does it sit in the
  pipeline relative to `contract_xref`, `join_frames`, FK-helper
  configuration, and `configure_workbook_view`?
* *The surface.* If declarative, what does the YAML or step
  configuration look like? (The existing examples remain illustrative.)
* *Outbound and inbound symmetry.* Both directions consume the same
  role classification; the FTR owns the mirror semantics for the
  reimport path.
* *The recommendation* on whether plugin support remains the preferred
  solution, per *Acceptance Criteria*.

The projection FTR explicitly does *not* own:

* the column-role taxonomy or its names,
* the reimport-survival rule for `row_identity` and `display_helper`,
* the metadata-source expectations for `contract_xref` /
  `configure_workbook_view` / FK-helper configuration /
  `join_frames`,
* editability, merge-back, lifecycle, or selector-language design.

== Verdict

* *Verdict:* *Refine.*
* *Not* keep-as-is: the FTR carries reconstructed role semantics that
  now live upstream and uses positional language that could foreclose
  the foundation's positional-neutral commitment.
* *Not* split: the FTR's open question (declarative-vs-plugin + the
  mechanism / surface) is a single well-shaped concern. Splitting
  outbound from inbound would re-introduce the asymmetry the driver
  review flagged; splitting decision from mechanism would orphan the
  *Acceptance Criteria*.
* *Not* superseded: the foundation FTR explicitly disclaims projection
  mechanics; the projection FTR is still the only home for that
  decision.

== Coordination notes

* The refinements proposed under R1-R7 should land *after* the
  foundation FTR is accepted, so that the *Depends on:* citation is
  not dangling. If the foundation FTR is rescoped or renamed during
  review, R1, R4, R6, and R7 inherit the new identifier; the rest
  are independent of the foundation's exact final shape.
* The same alignment pass on `FTR-DYNAMIC-VIEW-COLUMN-TARGETING-P5` is
  *not* performed by this artifact and is recommended as a follow-up.
  The two consumer FTRs are symmetric in their relationship to the
  foundation, so the targeting FTR likely receives an analogous set
  of edits (depend on foundation; targeting *by role* rather than via
  a new selector language; no role-taxonomy reconstruction; explicit
  Non-Goal for role-taxonomy work).
* No edits to the foundation FTR are recommended by this review. Its
  current text already names the role taxonomy and the metadata-source
  expectations that R1-R7 cite.

== Residual risks

* *Foundation FTR not yet accepted.* If the foundation is rescoped,
  renamed, or rejected, R1, R4, R6, and R7 must be re-evaluated.
  Mitigation: gate the refinement commit on the foundation FTR's
  acceptance.
* *Inbound-mirror scope expansion (R3).* Adding the inbound mirror
  slightly widens the projection FTR's scope. This is the right home
  per the driver review §7 and the symmetry is small, but reviewers
  should confirm the addition is welcome before refining.
* *Positional examples remain readable for the worldbuilding case.*
  R2 may make the FTR feel more abstract to a first-time reader.
  Mitigation: keep the YAML examples as illustrative material with an
  explicit role-mapping callout.
* *Drift between consumer FTRs.* If `FTR-DYNAMIC-VIEW-COLUMN-TARGETING-P5`
  is not aligned in a parallel pass, the two consumer FTRs may use
  different language for the same role concept.
