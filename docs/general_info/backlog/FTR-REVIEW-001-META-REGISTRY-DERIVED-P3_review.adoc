=== Important

*Sub-key distinction not captured in the registry.*

The two producers write into different children of the `derived.sheets.*`
namespace:

* `enrich_lookup._write_provenance` → `derived.sheets[<sheet>]["enrich_lookup"]`
* `fk_helpers._write_helper_provenance` → `derived.sheets[<sheet>]["helper_columns"]`

The registry entry describes `scope: workbook namespace with sheet-local
children under 'derived.sheets[<sheet>]'` but does not distinguish the two
sub-keys. This is fine for now. The risk: when C2 (FK consolidation) touches
`fk_helpers.py`, a refactor agent might not notice that `enrich_lookup` also
writes to `derived.sheets` under a *different* sub-key, and may assume the
channel is FK-only.

*Recommended fix* (before C2 starts): extend the `scope` field or add an
explicit note:

[source,yaml]
----
scope: |
  Workbook namespace with sheet-local children under `derived.sheets[<sheet>]`.
  Two independent sub-keys exist:
    - `helper_columns` — written by fk_helpers._write_helper_provenance
    - `enrich_lookup`  — written by enrich_lookup._write_provenance
  Consumers must treat each sub-key independently.
----

This pre-empts a silent cross-producer assumption during C2.
