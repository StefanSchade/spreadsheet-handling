==== Architectural Assessment

The slice does what the foundation is supposed to do: it gives the
load-bearing terms (`canonical`, `derived`, `operational-carrier`,
`model`, `presentation`, `workbook_identity`, `carrier`, `carrier-shape`,
and the data-layer `original` / `derived`) explicit definitions, makes
the layer × classification orthogonality visible, and stops the silent
drift that REVIEW-002 flagged.

Three properties make the slice work as a foundation rather than a
parallel vocabulary:

* The strict five-criterion definition of *canonical* in
  `02_meta_layer.adoc` names each historical expansion step
  (data→meta; intent vs realization; adapter portability;
  persistability; lifecycle decoupling) and is therefore falsifiable.
  Edge cases (presentation intent that is backend-restricted,
  read-path hints that become de-facto sources) can now be placed
  deliberately instead of absorbed.
* The data-layer vs meta-layer split in `01_data_and_meta.adoc` is
  surgical. It explicitly states that "canonical / non-canonical does
  not apply at the data layer" and gives the worked example (helper
  column data is derived; the meta claim about it is canonical). That
  one rule kills the most common conflation in older project text.
* The carrier text in `04_carrier.adoc` enumerates the five historical
  uses of "carrier", collapses (2) and (3) into *carrier-shape*, names
  (4) as the adapter object model rather than the carrier, and treats
  (5) (read-path hints) as a sub-mode of operational-carrier. The
  result is a definition that the live `carrier_artifact` and
  `read_path_hint` classifications map cleanly onto.

Scope discipline is good. The slice does not re-encode
`meta_registry.yaml`, does not touch behavior, does not redesign
`_meta.derived` lifecycle (which is owned by
`FTR-DERIVED-PROVENANCE-LIFECYCLE-P4A`), and defers re-encoding to a
separate future FTR explicitly in three places. The mapping tables make
it possible to read the live YAML through the foundation vocabulary
without touching the YAML now.

The ch04 concept doc is updated minimally (+7 lines, a single forward
reference into the new ch05 subsection). The functional-vs-temporal
framing in ch04 is preserved and now anchors into the foundation as
its operational definition — this is the correct relationship between
the two locations.

The glossary update introduces a *category* column and a `meta-system`
category that points back into `registry_semantics/` for full
definitions. The pattern keeps the glossary a short index rather than a
parallel definitional surface, which is the appropriate shape for a
foundation that lives elsewhere.
