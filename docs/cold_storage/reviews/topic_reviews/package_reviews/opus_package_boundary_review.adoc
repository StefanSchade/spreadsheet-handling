= Opus Package Boundary Review

== Verdict

The Sonnet inventory is broadly faithful: the 17 families it names map to the
code on disk, the public APIs match, and the meta-read/-write annotations
agree with what the implementations actually do.  Where Sonnet drifts is in
the refactoring candidates: a handful are framed as "consolidate near-duplicate
helpers" when the implementations are in fact materially different.  Two
candidates (001 cell primitives, 002 where predicates) survive review in a
narrowed form.  Most of the rest are cosmetic, premature, or papering over
real but out-of-scope contract questions.

The domain is *not* an architecture problem.  It is mostly an autonomy
problem: many transformations are already loosely coupled and could be
packaged with very little risk, while a smaller core (FK helpers, column
roles, the `_meta` namespace) is genuinely entangled and not a place to start.

Recommended action: do exactly one small, behaviour-preserving consolidation
slice as a warm-up, learn the test/CI cost, then revisit the broader plan.

== Audit Reliability

Reliable parts of the Sonnet audit:

* Public-API listings per family.  Spot-checks against every transformation
  module matched.
* Reads-meta / writes-meta annotations.  The `_meta` namespacing
  (`xref_crosstable`, `compact_multiaxis`, `cell_codecs`, `helper_policies`,
  `derived`, `sheets`, `workbook_view`, `frame_lifecycle`,
  `sparse_defaults`, `split_by_discriminator`) is exactly as documented.
* The architectural shape of `fk_helpers/` as a sub-package, with `policy.py`
  acting as a published contract surface re-exported via `__init__.py`.
* The dependency edges in `coupling_map.json`.  All edges I sampled exist as
  actual imports in the source.
* The "compact_multiaxis composes xref + cell_codec" structural claim:
  verified in `compact_multiaxis.py` imports.

Questionable parts of the Sonnet audit:

* Candidate 001 conflates three different primitives (`_is_empty_cell`,
  `_values_equal`, `_plain_value`) as a single "copy-paste cluster".  In fact
  only `_is_empty_cell` is verbatim identical across consumers.
  `_values_equal` is verbatim in four files but extended in
  `sparse_defaults.py` (short-circuit on missing scalars).  `_plain_value` has
  *at least three* incompatible semantics across files (Timestamp→ISO in
  `discriminator_split/values.py`; ""-on-empty in `tabular_views.py`;
  None-on-empty in the validations files).  A naïve "extract to shared
  primitive" would silently change behaviour.
* Candidate 002 claims the `where` engine in `join_views.py`,
  `extractions/frame_extract.py` and `validations/reference_validations.py`
  is "close but not identical".  The first two are in fact verbatim copies
  of each other; the third (`_condition_mask`) is materially different - it
  normalises with `_format_scalar`/`_plain_value`, supports
  `optional_missing`, and integrates with `enabled_when` switch frames.  The
  candidate is sound for the join_views ↔ extractions pair, but is not a
  three-way unification.
* Candidate 004 ("unify the two Finding dataclasses") understates the
  divergence.  `domain/finding_frame.py::Finding` is a tabular-row serializer;
  `domain/validations/findings.py::Finding` is a severity-policy input.  The
  module docstring of `finding_frame.py` already says collapsing them would
  flatten genuinely different diagnostic semantics.  This is a naming
  collision, not a model redundancy.
* The executive summary's "three safest first slices" leads with 001, 002,
  `004`.  Only the narrowed forms of 001 and 002 are actually safe.  `004` should
  not be touched until someone has a behaviour-level reason to.

Out of scope but worth flagging:

* The `dense_axes` `NotImplementedError` placeholder in
  `compact_multiaxis.py` is correctly noted by Sonnet but not actionable.
* The "interim" status of `column_roles/resolver.py` (planned migration to
  `_meta.derived.sheets.*.column_roles`) is a real upcoming change; any
  refactor of `column_roles`, `project_by_role`, or `validate_columns` now
  is wasted work.

== Natural Transformation Families

The following families are genuinely autonomous at the domain level - their
direct imports outside `pandas`/stdlib are limited to a shared writer of
`_meta.frame_lifecycle` or the seven-column finding frame:

* `xref_crosstable` - no domain-internal imports; the only meta it reads is
  its own namespace (and its own dense-axis writeback).
* `cell_codec` - reads `_meta.legend_blocks` (an external sidecar input,
  not produced inside the domain).
* `discriminator_split` package - self-contained; its own internal
  `values.py` core is already isolated, with a docstring that explicitly
  refuses promotion to a broad shared utility.
* `sparse_defaults` - reads `_meta.xref_crosstable` only when auto-detecting
  columns; an explicit `columns` parameter makes it standalone.
* `join_views`, `tabular_views`, `extractions` - each depends only on
  `frame_lifecycle` and pandas.
* `resource_overrides` - depends only on `finding_frame`.
* Writer steps (`structured_yaml`, `key_value_writer`, `artifact_manifest`)
  - stdlib + yaml only.
* `meta_bootstrap` / `yaml_overrides` - meta plumbing only.
* `finding_frame`, `frame_lifecycle` - the shared utilities themselves.

These could be split into clearer top-level domain packages later with very
low risk.  None of them, however, are *under pressure* to split.  The
package-boundary win available today is consolidation of duplicated
primitives across them, not re-packaging them.

== Coupling Hotspots

Real coupling (verified by imports and meta reads):

. `fk_helpers/policy.py` is the cluster's contract surface.  It is imported by
  `column_roles/resolver.py`, `transformations/derived_column_policy.py`,
  `transformations/helpers.py` (for `reorder_helpers_next_to_fk`), and
  `validations/fk_helpers.py`.  Touching the policy reader shape will ripple
  across four packages.
. `column_roles` is read by `project_by_role` and `validate_columns` and is
  internally bound to `fk_helpers/policy` for derived-helper detection.
  Three packages are pinned to a module that the implementation itself
  describes as "interim".
. `_meta` is a shared mutable dict and the dominant coupling medium.
  Sonnet correctly identifies this.  Cross-step coupling that looks like
  "no import" is actually "implicit meta-key contract" in many places:
  `sparse_defaults` ↔ `xref_crosstable`, `project_by_role` ↔
  `xref_crosstable`/`workbook_view`/`sheets`/`derived`,
  `derived_column_policy` ↔ `enrich_lookup`/`fk_helpers`/`workbook_view`.
. `transformations/helpers.py` is a structural anomaly.  Four of its public
  names (`mark_helpers`, `clean_aux_columns`, `flatten_headers`,
  `unflatten_headers`) are pure header/prefix utilities with no FK
  knowledge.  A fifth (`reorder_helpers_next_to_fk`) imports from
  `fk_helpers`.  The whole file is a sibling of the `fk_helpers/` package,
  not a member of it.  The pipeline registry pins symbol paths to this
  module (see `pipeline/registry.py:34..42`), so a move is not zero-cost.

Coupling that looks scary in the audit but is actually low-risk:

* `validations/graph_validations` depending on
  `validations/reference_validations` for `ReferenceFinding`/
  `findings_to_frame` is intra-family by design.
* `derived_column_policy` depending on `finding_frame` and
  `fk_helpers/policy` is the cleanup pair of `enrich_helpers`/
  `enrich_lookup` and a legitimate domain edge.
* `compact_multiaxis` depending on `xref_crosstable` and `cell_codec` is
  *the* definition of the family ("compose two primitives") and should be
  preserved as-is.

== Refactoring Candidate Assessment

| Candidate | Assessment | Reason

| [[OPB-001-CELL-PRIMITIVES]]001 cell primitives
| accept (narrowed)
| Extract only `_is_empty_cell` (verbatim identical across 5+ files) and the
  bare `_values_equal` (verbatim in cell_codec, xref_crosstable,
  resource_overrides, discriminator_split/values).  Do *not* include
  `_plain_value`: at least three incompatible semantics exist
  (Timestamp→ISO; ""-on-empty; None-on-empty).  Do *not* migrate the
  extended `_values_equal` in `sparse_defaults.py` in this slice.  No public
  API, no test imports of these names.

| [[OPB-002-WHERE-PREDICATES]]002 where predicate engine
| accept (narrowed)
| `join_views._apply_where` and `extractions/frame_extract._apply_where` are
  verbatim copies.  Extract those two only.  `reference_validations.
  _condition_mask` is *not* the same function (different normalisation,
  optional_missing, switch-frame integration) and should be left local in
  this slice.  Sonnet's claim of three-way duplication is wrong.

| [[OPB-003-SPLIT-HELPERS]]003 split helpers.py
| defer
| Correct conceptually: `helpers.py` mixes FK-aware reordering with
  header/prefix utilities that have nothing to do with FKs.  But the
  pipeline registry hardcodes symbol paths to
  `domain.transformations.helpers:flatten_headers` and friends
  (`pipeline/registry.py`).  Moving the module is a public-edge change for
  any YAML pipeline file that references those targets.  Worth doing, but
  not first.

| [[OPB-004-FINDING-CLASSES]]004 unify the two Finding classes
| reject (for now)
| Real naming collision, not real model redundancy.  The two dataclasses
  serve different consumers and `finding_frame.py`'s own docstring states
  that collapsing them would erase semantic distinctions.  A rename-only
  change is mechanical noise; a model collapse needs a separate FTR.

| [[OPB-005-LEGEND-GROUPS]]005 move legend_groups to cell_codec
| defer
| The duplication is real but localised to two files (`compact_multiaxis`,
  `cell_codec`) and small (~30 LOC).  Moving it requires choosing whether
  the shared API returns `(token, group)` pairs or two functions; that is a
  cell_codec public-API decision and out of scope for a tidy-up slice.

| [[OPB-006-FK-HELPERS-RAISE-VS-RETURN]]006 validate_fk_helpers raise vs return
| defer
| Internal contract clarity only.  No observable behaviour change is
  possible without picking a new contract.  Belongs in the FK helpers
  cluster review, not in a package-boundary slice.

| [[OPB-007-DEAD-PREFIX]]007 dead `prefix` parameter
| defer
| One-line deprecation warning or docstring fix.  Worth doing; not a
  refactoring slice.  Could be a trivial follow-up commit.

| [[OPB-008-SCHEMA-MAINTENANCE-WRITEINTENT]]008 schema_maintenance WriteIntent

| Enforcing DRY_RUN is a behaviour change in a high-risk subsystem

== Recommended First Slice

Slice::
Extract `_is_empty_cell` and the bare-bones `_values_equal` into a private
shared module `src/spreadsheet_handling/domain/_cell_primitives.py` and
migrate the verbatim-identical call sites only.

Why this slice::
+
* Behaviour is preserved by construction: only verbatim-identical
  implementations are folded together.
* Public API impact is zero - both names are leading-underscore private
  and no test imports them.
* Touches files across the domain (xref, cell_codec, join_views,
  tabular_views, extractions, reference_validations, graph_validations,
  resource_overrides) so it doubles as a low-stakes smoke test for the
  validation command itself.
* It is small enough to be reverted as one PR if anything surprising
  surfaces.
* It deliberately *does not* preempt the FK helpers cluster or the
  column_roles "interim" rework.

Expected risk::
very low.  The risk is dominated by Python import cycles, which are
mechanically detectable.  The new module imports only `pandas` and
`numbers`; nothing in the domain imports it back.

Expected test impact::
none on signatures, none on tests.  The existing test command
(`.venv/bin/pytest -m 'not legacy and not prehex and not slow' tests/unit
tests/integration tests/roundtrip`) should remain `1087 passed, 1 xfailed`.
If a single assertion shifts, the slice is wrong; revert it.

Files likely involved::
+
* New: `src/spreadsheet_handling/domain/_cell_primitives.py` (or
  `cell_primitives.py` - see open question).
* Touched (replace local def with import):
  ** `domain/transformations/cell_codec.py` (lines ~559, ~566)
  ** `domain/transformations/xref_crosstable.py` (lines ~271, ~734)
  ** `domain/transformations/join_views.py` (line ~512)
  ** `domain/transformations/tabular_views.py` (line ~365) - only
     `_is_empty_cell`; leave the local `_plain_value`.
  ** `domain/transformations/resource_overrides.py` (line ~443)
  ** `domain/extractions/frame_extract.py` (line ~272)
  ** `domain/validations/reference_validations.py` (line ~720) - only
     `_is_empty_cell`; leave the local `_plain_value`.
  ** `domain/validations/graph_validations.py` (line ~409) - only
     `_is_empty_cell`.
* Explicitly *not* touched in this slice:
  ** `domain/transformations/sparse_defaults.py` (extended `_values_equal`).
  ** `domain/transformations/discriminator_split/values.py` (its docstring
     already declares it a package-local core).
  ** `domain/structured_yaml.py` (different needs around YAML scalar
     rendering).

Public API impact::
none.

Expected behaviour change::
none.

== Defer Explicitly

The following should not be touched as part of a package-boundary effort
right now:

* The entire FK helpers cluster (`domain/transformations/fk_helpers/`,
  `domain/validations/fk_helpers.py`, `domain/fk_relations.py`,
  `domain/helper_policies.py`).  Per the brief: treat as a later cluster
  review, and the cluster is already packaged.
* `domain/column_roles/`.  The resolver is explicitly an interim
  implementation; refactoring it now will be obsoleted by the planned
  `_meta.derived.sheets.*.column_roles` channel.
* `domain/transformations/project_by_role.py` and
  `domain/validations/validate_columns.py`.  Both are pinned to
  `column_roles`; they should move with it.
* `domain/schema_maintenance/`.  High blast radius (it edits `_meta`
  references across all known root names); WriteIntent semantics are
  unresolved.  Out of scope.
* `domain/workbook_views.configure_workbook_view`.  Its deferred-execution
  sub-language (`where`, `sort_by`, `join`, `pivot`) couples to the
  rendering pipeline that is out of scope for this audit.
* The two `Finding` dataclasses.  Their coexistence is intentional and
  documented in `finding_frame.py`.
* `compact_multiaxis` legend-group consolidation (Candidate 005).  Small,
  localised, not load-bearing.
* `transformations/helpers.py` split (Candidate 003).  Worth doing *after*
  001/002 land and the pipeline registry impact is measured.

== Open Questions

These are the questions that actually block the first or second slice.

. Naming convention for a new private domain primitive module.  Is it
  `_cell_primitives.py` (leading underscore, package-private convention),
  or a bare `cell_primitives.py` re-exported from `domain/__init__.py`?
  The current codebase prefers leading-underscore *symbols* but not
  leading-underscore *modules*; the answer affects how future shared
  utilities are added.
. Is the extended `_values_equal` in `sparse_defaults.py` (with the
  `_is_missing_scalar` short-circuit) intentional, or accidental drift?
  Answer determines whether `sparse_defaults` joins the shared primitive
  in a follow-up slice or stays divergent on purpose.
. Should `transformations/discriminator_split/values.py` ever migrate to the
  shared primitive module?  Its docstring says no; the question is whether
  that policy is durable or was a moment-in-time choice.
. For Slice 2 (where engine consolidation, narrowed to join_views ↔
  extractions): should the shared module also accept an injectable
  `is_empty` predicate so it can be used by `reference_validations` later,
  or is that premature design?

== Final Recommendation

Do Slice 1 (extract `_is_empty_cell` and the bare `_values_equal` only) as a
single small PR.  Run the documented test command.  Confirm `1087 passed,
1 xfailed`.  Stop there for the session.

The natural Slice 2, once Slice 1 is green, is the narrowed Candidate 002:
extract the verbatim `_apply_where` shared between `join_views` and
`extractions` only.  Defer everything else until after those two slices
have proven the test loop is stable.

Do not start a "domain package re-organisation" effort.  The package
boundaries that exist today are mostly defensible; the genuine
deduplication wins are small and can be taken one at a time without any
structural commitment.
