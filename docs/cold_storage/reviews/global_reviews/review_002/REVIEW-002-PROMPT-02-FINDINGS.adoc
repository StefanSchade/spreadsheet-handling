=== Cross-cutting facts that drive the dispositions

. *Registry coupling is string-only.* All 11 modules are bound to the pipeline
exclusively by colon-target strings resolved via `importlib` in `steps.py`.
There are *no static import edges from `pipeline/` into these modules*. Moving
a module into a package and re-exporting its registry symbol from the package
root is therefore behavior-preserving for the registry -- the favorable
precondition the `fk_helpers`/`discriminator_split` splits already proved.
. *One intra-family static import cluster.* `compact_multiaxis.py` statically
imports `expand_xref`/`contract_xref` from `xref_crosstable` and
`decode_cell_values`/`encode_cell_values` from `cell_codec`. These three are a
*coupled triad*, not three independent modules (§2.3, §2.8, §3).
[[P2-GUARD-FAMILY-GAP]]
. *The guarded boundary family does not yet cover these 11.* The
`tests/architecture/domain_transformations/` guards (cross-family imports,
package public-API, registry-uses-public-API, re-export) enumerate only
`fk_helpers` and `discriminator_split`. The 11 are constrained today only by
the directory-level layer guards and the canonical-meta hardening guards. Any
split of one of these 11 must add an analogous re-export guard *as part of the
split*, exactly as the discriminator analysis prescribes.
. *Canonical, roundtrip-relevant meta is pinned.* The hardening guard pins
`xref_crosstable`, `cell_codecs`, `compact_multiaxis`, `helper_policies`,
`workbook_view`, `workbook_view.sheet_mappings` as
`(meta_canonical, canonical_meta)` -- i.e. persisted and roundtrip-bearing.
The meta payload is the carrier of the symmetry invariants; it cannot be split
away from the functions that produce/consume it.
[[P2-FINDING-MODEL-QUADRUPLICATION]]
. *Finding-model duality is actually quadruplication.* Pass 1's D-005 named
two finding models. Pass 2 evidence: `reference_validations.py`,
`resource_overrides.py`, and `derived_column_policy.py` each independently
define `FINDING_COLUMNS` + a frozen `*Finding` dataclass + `to_record` +
`findings_to_frame` + `_failure_message`, in addition to
`domain/validations/findings.py::Finding`. This is the single most concrete
new architectural observation in this pass (§4, §6).
[[P2-WHERE-DSL-DUPLICATION-WATCH]]
. *A second recurring duplication family: a `where`/condition predicate DSL.*
`reference_validations._condition_mask` and `join_views._apply_where` share
the *identical* predicate vocabulary (`equals/in/non_empty/is_null/not_null`)
implemented twice. This is distinct from the tiny-helper duplication Pass 1
flagged. It is *adjacent, independent* duplication, not shared infrastructure
(§4).

== 2. Per-Module Architectural Assessments

=== 2.1 `domain/transformations/xref_crosstable.py` (801 LOC)

*Surface.* `expand_xref`, `contract_xref` (registry steps `expand_xref` /
`contract_xref`). No `__all__`. Imported intra-family by `compact_multiaxis`.
Meta key `xref_crosstable` (canonical, pinned).

*Responsibilities.* (1) matrix->relation expansion; (2) relation->matrix
contraction; (3) a large *dense-axes resolution subsystem*
(`_dense_axes_from_config_or_meta`, `_resolve_dense_axes`,
`_axis_source_config`, `_row_identities_from_axis_source`,
`_column_keys_from_axis_source`, `_ensure_relation_within_dense_axes`,
`_stored_*`, `_dense_axes_meta_payload` -- ~lines 360-731, ≈46% of the file,
the FTR-XREF-DENSE-AXES-P4A2 feature); (4) meta read/write
(`_xref_config`/`_write_xref_meta`); (5) generic equality/normalization and
schema-guard helpers.

*Internal coupling.* `expand_xref`/`contract_xref` are joined by the shared
`_meta.xref_crosstable` payload (column order, dense config). The dense-axes
subsystem is entered *only from the contract path* and is internally cohesive;
`expand_xref` only carries a previous `dense_axes` block forward. The subsystem
shares only the tiny equality/normalization helpers and the
`_require_frame`/`_ensure_*`/`_as_list` validators with the core.

*External / registry coupling.* pandas-only; no `core`/`rendering` imports
(clean leaf). Registry: string colon-target only. `compact_multiaxis` imports
both public functions directly -- a split must preserve package-root
re-exports.

*Candidate seams.* One real seam: `xref_crosstable/{core,dense_axes,_meta}`.
`core` = expand/contract + payload schema; `dense_axes` = the resolution
subsystem (only contract calls into it); `_meta`/helpers = the shared tiny
core. Boundary is the existing `_dense_axes_from_config_or_meta` +
`_resolve_dense_axes` + `_ensure_relation_within_dense_axes` +
`_dense_axes_meta_payload` call surface.

*Risk.* `contract_xref(expand_xref(x)) == x` is a tested bijection; the meta
payload is its carrier. Splitting `expand` from `contract`, or splitting the
meta writer away from either, would break the symmetry contract. Dense-axes
*influences row/column ordering that the roundtrip depends on*, so the
`core<->dense_axes` boundary must be an explicit internal API, not an implicit
one. Conflict/precedence of dense reconstruction is reserved by P6 -- a
dense-axes split must not grow a conflict module.

*Disposition.* Largest module; the dense-axes seam is real, FTR-scoped, and
roughly half the file -- the single highest-value behavior-preserving target.
But the seam crosses the symmetry contract and the canonical meta carrier, so
it is feasible *only under constraints* (keep expand+contract+meta together as
`core`; dense_axes as a sibling with an explicit internal API; package-root
re-exports for both public functions; a re-export guard and a
`contract(expand(x))==x` + dense-shape witness added in the same slice).
*Classification: PROCEED WITH CONSTRAINTS.*

*Future-FTR outline.* `FTR-XREF-CROSSTABLE-DENSE-AXES-PACKAGE-SPLIT`:
verbatim-move `core`/`dense_axes`/`_meta` per the discriminator template;
re-export guard; roundtrip + dense-shape witnesses; explicit non-goal:
no conflict/precedence module (P6).

*Non-goals.* No splitting expand from contract. No extracting the meta writer.
No anticipatory dense-reconstruction conflict resolver.

=== 2.2 `domain/validations/reference_validations.py` (~725 LOC)

*Surface.* `validate_references`, `findings_to_frame`, `ReferenceFinding`,
`FINDING_COLUMNS` (registry step `validate_references`). Re-exported by
`domain/validations/__init__.py`; `FINDING_COLUMNS`/finding model also used by
`graph_validations.py`. No meta-registry producer/consumer (lowest metadata
coupling, tied with §2.7).

*Responsibilities.* (1) public orchestration (mode/findings/fail-raise);
(2) rule dispatch; (3) four independent rule validators (`unique`,
`primary_key`, `foreign_key`, `unique_reference`); (4) a *condition predicate
DSL* (`enabled_when`/`when`, `_condition_mask`, ≈175 LOC); (5) its own finding
model + formatting; (6) generic value helpers.

*Internal coupling.* The four validators are *parallel and independent* (none
calls another); all share `_frame_and_columns`/`_optional_frame`/`_apply_when`/
`_duplicate_findings`/`_schema_finding` and the finding model. The condition
DSL is cross-cutting infrastructure used by all four plus the dispatcher.

*External / registry coupling.* pandas-only leaf. The finding model is the
real coupling: it is shared *cross-module* with `graph_validations.py` and runs
parallel to `domain/validations/findings.py::Finding` (D-005).

*Candidate seams.* Internally clean: condition-DSL; the four validators;
the finding model. *But the finding-model seam is not local* -- it is one of
four parallel finding models. Extracting the condition DSL as a standalone
package would weaken locality (single consumer).

*Risk.* No symmetry/bijection; no meta carrier -- low *behavioral* risk. The
architectural risk is the opposite of a split risk: packaging this module now
would harden the boundary of *one of four* finding models, entrenching D-005
quadruplication and pre-empting the consolidation that should come first.

*Disposition.* Mechanically the easiest internal split of all 11 (independent
validators, clean DSL seam), and the second-largest file -- but the dominant
architectural question (finding-model quadruplication) is unresolved and
*upstream* of any packaging decision. Splitting before D-005 is decided is
churn that makes the eventual consolidation harder.
*Classification: ANALYZE FIRST* (resolve D-005 ownership/consolidation first;
the internal seams are clear and can wait).

*Future-FTR outline.* `FTR-FINDING-MODEL-CONSOLIDATION` (cross-module,
prerequisite) -> then optionally
`FTR-REFERENCE-VALIDATIONS-PACKAGE-SPLIT` (condition-DSL + per-rule modules
*within* a package, not as standalone utils).

*Non-goals.* No standalone "predicate-DSL" or "validation-engine" package. No
packaging this module before D-005 is decided.

=== 2.3 `domain/transformations/cell_codec.py` (631 LOC)

*Surface.* `parse_cell_value`, `serialize_cell_value`, `decode_cell_values`,
`encode_cell_values`, `ParsedCellValue` (registry steps `decode_cell_values` /
`encode_cell_values`). Imported intra-family by `compact_multiaxis`. Meta key
`cell_codecs` (canonical, pinned).

*Responsibilities.* (1) the scalar codec inverse pair
`parse_cell_value`/`serialize_cell_value` + `ParsedCellValue` + normalization;
(2) thin frame wrappers `decode_cell_values`/`encode_cell_values`;
(3) allowed-value resolution incl. `_meta.legend_blocks` lookup
(`_allowed_values`/`_legend_tokens`); (4) meta write; (5) generic helpers.

*Internal coupling.* The scalar codec is the inseparable core; the frame
transforms are ~80 LOC wrappers that call it row-wise. The legend resolution
straddles both layers and depends on a cross-module `_meta.legend_blocks`
contract (produced elsewhere).

*External / registry coupling.* pandas-only leaf. Participates in a
*cross-module* lossless chain `expand_xref -> decode -> encode ->
contract_xref` (tested) -- a bijection co-owned with `xref_crosstable`.

*Candidate seams.* A scalar-vs-frame seam exists but is thin and asymmetric;
both halves share the inverse-pair invariant, the legend contract, and the
meta carrier. No clean acyclic cut.

*Risk.* `serialize(parse(x))` is an inverse pair; splitting the pair, or
splitting the legend resolver from the codec, breaks lossless roundtrip.
Splitting yields two tiny modules sharing an invariant + contract -- net
negative for locality.

*Disposition.* Large but *inherently cohesive*: a codec is one concept. The
seam is thin, the invariants are dense, and it is the binding member of the
triad (§3). 631 LOC is the smallest of the "large" group and is justified by
intrinsic complexity (modes, normalization, legend, canonical order).
*Classification: DO NOT SPLIT.*

*Future-FTR outline.* None recommended. If `xref_crosstable` is packaged,
`cell_codec` stays a single module; only its package-root re-export needs to
remain stable for `compact_multiaxis`.

*Non-goals.* No scalar/frame package split. No extracting legend resolution
into a shared package (it is codec-specific despite the name collision with
`compact_multiaxis._legend_groups` -- see §4).

=== 2.4 `domain/transformations/join_views.py` (567 LOC)

*Surface.* `join_frames` only (registry step `join_frames`). Imports
`domain.frame_lifecycle` (domain->domain, allowed). Produces `frame_lifecycle`.

*Responsibilities.* One operation with intrinsically combinatorial
sub-concerns: key-config resolution (six spellings), column selection +
rename + collision suffixing, a `where` predicate DSL, semi/left/inner merge,

*Internal coupling.* Strictly linear: every helper serves the single
`join_frames` entrypoint. No sub-helper is reused by another concern; no
inverse.

*External / registry coupling.* pandas + `frame_lifecycle`. String
colon-target only; no external imports of its internals.

`where` DSL shared-in-spirit with `reference_validations` (§4) -- a
consolidation question, explicitly *not* a split question.

*Risk.* Low behavioral risk; no symmetry. The risk is fragmentation: a package
split would scatter one cohesive operation into single-consumer micro-modules.

*Disposition.* Large because the join feature is intrinsically combinatorial
(key spellings x column selection x collision policy x where x join kind x
lifecycle), not because it is overloaded. *Classification: DO NOT SPLIT.*

*Future-FTR outline.* None. The `where`/predicate-DSL duplication with
`reference_validations` is a *watch item*, not an FTR, until a third consumer
appears (§4).

*Non-goals.* No join "engine"/"strategy" framework. No premature predicate-DSL
extraction.

home/guard-family. *Classification: ANALYZE FIRST* (needs a discriminator-style

resolver. No tiny shared-util package.

== 8. Final Summary

*Which modules are the highest-value refactor targets?*
`xref_crosstable.py` (rank 1, ready under constraints) and `workbook_views.py`
(rank 2, ready but FTR-timed). Both have a *real*, contract-or-FTR-scoped seam
that the already-proven discriminator/fk_helpers methodology fits exactly.
`reference_validations.py` is high-value but only *after* finding-model
consolidation -- and that consolidation (`FTR-FINDING-MODEL-CONSOLIDATION`) is
itself the highest conceptual-integrity win in this pass.

*Which modules are deceptively complex and should remain cohesive?*
`cell_codec`, `compact_multiaxis`, `join_views`, `tabular_views`,
`resource_overrides` (§7). Their size is inherent. The triad
(`xref`/`cell_codec`/`compact_multiaxis`) must be reasoned about as one
bijection in three files, never as three independent split candidates.

*Which seams are mature enough now?*
Exactly one is unconstrained-mature in shape: `workbook_views`
configure<->readback, mediated by a stable canonical contract -- but its
*timing* is constrained by active P4A/P6 ownership of that contract. The
`xref_crosstable` core<->dense_axes seam is mature and FTR-bounded but crosses
the symmetry carrier, so it is "mature under explicit constraints". No other
seam among the 11 is both mature and local.

*Which areas still lack architectural clarity?*
(1) Finding-model quadruplication (D-005 escalated) -- ownership and target
shape undecided. (2) The helper-policy/derived subsystem
(`helper_policies`/`enrich_lookup`/`derived_column_policy`/`fk_helpers`):
contract ownership is split across in-flight P4A/P6 FTRs and `helper_policies`'
architectural *home* (why it sits outside `transformations/`, where its guards
belong) is unresolved.

*Which future FTRs are worthwhile vs churn?*
Worthwhile: finding-model consolidation; the xref and workbook-view splits
(discriminator-template, guarded, witness-backed); the helper-policy subsystem
*analysis*. Churn: any split of the five cohesive modules; any predicate-DSL /
util / engine / conflict framework; packaging reference_validations before
consolidation; touching enrich_lookup/derived_column_policy ahead of their
FTRs.

*Accidental vs inherent complexity (the headline question).*
The maintainability risk in these 11 modules is *predominantly accidental and
cheaply repayable*, sitting on top of genuinely inherent domain complexity:

* *Inherent (do not "fix"):* the xref/codec/compact bijections and their meta
carriers; the workbook render<->reimport reverse mapping; the
inline-vs-policy reconciliation in enrich_lookup; pivot/join combinatorics.
The layered model + canonical-meta registry is a *proportionate* response, not
over-design. Splitting these would *increase* architectural drift.
* *Accidental (repayable, no architecture change):* feature-first module size
in `xref_crosstable`/`workbook_views`; finding-model quadruplication;
predicate-DSL and tiny-helper duplication; the unresolved `helper_policies`
placement. None requires a new framework; all are addressable with the
existing, proven, guard-backed split/consolidation methodology.

Net: the hotspots are real but *bounded and named*. Two are ready for
behavior-preserving splits under explicit constraints; one consolidation
(finding models) is the highest-leverage conceptual-integrity fix and gates a
third; the remaining modules are inherently cohesive and should be explicitly
protected from decomposition. This is consistent with Pass 1's
"stable-beta architecture with localized, named, non-architectural residue"
verdict -- Pass 2 makes that residue specific, ranked, and FTR-actionable.
