===== FTR-META-REGISTRY-P3H — Add a manual meta registry with behaviour profiles

*Depends-on:* <<FTR-SPREADSHEET-META-SEMANTICS-P3H#,FTR-SPREADSHEET-META-SEMANTICS-P3H>>

*Context:* After the write-path split between `meta_canonical` and `meta_rendering` is clarified, the codebase still needs an explicit inventory of the meta entries that already exist. Today, too much of that knowledge is still distributed across free-form keys, ad-hoc conventions, and local special cases.

*Problem statement:* Without a structured manual registry, the new terminology would remain largely prose-level guidance. Future work on precedence, scope inheritance, conflict handling, XLSX alignment, or later typed models would still depend on reverse-engineering scattered behavior from code.

*Goals:*

. Capture the existing spreadsheet-relevant meta entries in a structured, manually curated registry.
. Record for each entry a concise behaviour profile along the write path.
. Make scope, origin, persistence, roundtrip relevance, producer, consumer, precedence, and inheritance/merge expectations explicit.
. Create a foundation for later targeted IR resolvers and eventual typing without committing to either yet.
. Improve reviewability when new meta entries are introduced or old ones change meaning.

*Acceptance criteria:*

. Add a structured registry artifact (JSON, YAML, or equivalent) for current spreadsheet-relevant meta entries.
. Each entry records at least: name, meaning, scope, origin, render relevance, persistence behavior, roundtrip relevance, producer, and consumer.
. Where applicable, entries also record scope inheritance, merge behavior, and precedence/conflict notes.
. Add brief documentation explaining how the registry relates to `meta_canonical`, `meta_rendering`, and `RenderPlan`.
. Seed the registry with an initial pass over the already known meta entries rather than leaving it as an empty framework.

*Non-goals:*

- No code generation from the registry in this FTR.
- No full typed meta model in this FTR.
- No generic IR resolver framework in this FTR.
- No requirement that every registry field already drive runtime behavior.

*Why now:* Before a second spreadsheet adapter and before stronger automation, the system needs an explicit, reviewable inventory of what its meta already means.
