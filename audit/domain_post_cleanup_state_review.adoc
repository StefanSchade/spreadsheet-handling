= Domain Post-Cleanup Closing-State Review

Closing architectural / readability review of
``src/spreadsheet_handling/domain`` after the cleanup and package-formation
waves landed on ``main``. Read-only audit; no source files were modified.

Inputs consulted: ``src/spreadsheet_handling/domain``,
``src/spreadsheet_handling/pipeline`` (registry + step bindings only),
test imports under ``tests/unit``, ``tests/integration``, ``tests/roundtrip``,
and the prior audit artifacts (``transformation_inventory.adoc``,
``coupling_map.json``, ``refactoring_candidates.adoc``,
``opus_package_boundary_review.adoc``, ``package_formation_review.adoc``,
``enrich_lookup_package_review.adoc``, ``cell_codec_package_review.adoc``,
``compact_multiaxis_package_review.adoc``,
``legend_reader_consolidation_review.adoc``,
``domain_cleanup_wave_review.adoc``).

The standalone "documentation-blind domain audit findings" file is not
present under ``audit/`` in this snapshot (its findings landed via the
inventory + candidates artifacts and the Opus boundary review); this review
proceeds without it.

== Verdict

Green

The waves delivered what the earlier audits proposed without overreach:
verbatim duplication removed, two large transformation modules split into
small packages, one shared legend reader consolidated, no public API or
``_meta`` shape changed, and the test baseline holds at ``1087 passed, 1
xfailed``. The deferred items (FK helpers cluster, ``column_roles``
interim resolver, ``schema_maintenance`` write-intent question, the dual
``Finding`` dataclasses, the ``helpers.py`` split) are still deferred for
documented reasons. Nothing on disk argues for stopping the wave on a
regression note; the only argument for "Yellow" would be the remaining
density in ``validations/reference_validations.py`` and
``schema_maintenance/meta_update.py``, both of which were explicitly
out of scope and remain so by policy.

== Structural Health

Yes - structurally healthier than the pre-wave baseline.

Concrete signals visible in the tree:

* Two new behaviour-preserving packages
  (``transformations/xref_crosstable/``,
  ``transformations/enrich_lookup/``) replace what used to be two of the
  five largest flat modules. Both expose only their pre-wave public
  symbols via a minimal ``__init__.py`` re-export
  (``contract_xref``/``expand_xref``; ``enrich_lookup``).
* One third package (``transformations/cell_codec/``) landed with a
  clean scalar/frame split. ``__init__.py`` re-exports exactly the five
  pre-wave public symbols.
* Three private shared utilities live at the domain root or under
  ``transformations/``:
  ** ``domain/_cell_primitives.py`` (35 LOC) - leaf with two functions
     (``_is_empty_cell``, ``_values_equal``), depends only on
     ``pandas``/``typing``; importers traced across 13 sites including
     transformations, validations, the two new packages, and
     ``structured_yaml.py``.
  ** ``domain/_where_predicates.py`` (104 LOC) - depends only on
     ``_cell_primitives``; importers are exactly the three sites the
     earlier audit identified (``extractions/frame_extract``,
     ``transformations/join_views``,
     ``transformations/tabular_views``).
  ** ``domain/transformations/_legend_blocks.py`` (70 LOC) - depends
     only on ``_cell_primitives``; imported by both
     ``cell_codec/scalar.py`` and ``transformations/compact_multiaxis.py``.
* All shared utilities are leading-underscore private modules. Their
  imports are one-way leaves. None re-enters the transformation tree
  upward.
* No new ``__init__.py`` re-exports cross-package internals. Internal
  helpers stay private behind the package wall they live in.
* Registry strings (``pipeline/registry.py`` lines 90-149) and test
  imports continue to resolve through the new package facades unchanged.

Three import-graph properties earlier reviews flagged as risks are now
visibly absent:

. The xref ``operation``/``dense_axes`` load-order workaround is gone -
  the package owns a small ``primitives.py`` leaf that both sides import.
. ``cell_codec`` split without needing a third ``primitives.py`` (frame
  imports scalar in one direction).
. ``enrich_lookup`` split without needing a third submodule (operation
  imports policy in one direction).

== Visible Improvements

The package-formation pattern is now a small but consistent template
across the transformations layer:

* ``__init__.py`` is a 2-3 line re-export of the documented public
  surface only, with an explicit ``__all__``.
* Behaviour-bearing code sits in one or more sibling modules named for
  their concern (``operation``, ``dense_axes``, ``primitives``,
  ``scalar``, ``frame``, ``policy``).
* Private helpers and constants are package-local by location and
  underscore convention.

Concrete code-layout improvements:

* The ~785-LOC flat ``xref_crosstable.py`` is gone. In its place:
  ``operation.py`` (337 LOC, the narrative), ``dense_axes.py`` (397
  LOC, the optional reconstruction concern), ``primitives.py`` (105
  LOC, shared leaves), and a 3-line ``__init__.py``. Readers who do
  not use dense axes no longer scroll past them.
* The ~615-LOC flat ``cell_codec.py`` is gone. ``scalar.py`` (300 LOC)
  and ``frame.py`` (297 LOC) cleanly separate per-cell parse/serialize
  from DataFrame projection. ``ParsedCellValue`` sits with the scalar
  publics; ``_META_KEY = "cell_codecs"`` sits with the frame publics.
* The ~567-LOC flat ``enrich_lookup.py`` is gone. ``operation.py``
  (398 LOC) holds the orchestrator plus per-call guards;
  ``policy.py`` (197 LOC) holds the seven ``_resolve_*`` resolvers
  that reconcile inline arguments against
  ``_meta.helper_policies.lookup.<lookup>``.
* Legend-block reading is now one function
  (``_read_legend_block``). ``cell_codec/scalar._legend_tokens`` and
  ``compact_multiaxis._legend_groups`` are both thin wrappers around
  it. Five error texts are now defined once.
* The verbatim ``_is_empty_cell`` / ``_values_equal`` duplications
  identified by the inventory (eight files) are gone; the small set of
  intentional divergences (``sparse_defaults`` extended
  ``_values_equal``, ``discriminator_split/values.py``
  package-local core, ``structured_yaml`` YAML-render needs) remain
  documented and unchanged.
* The bounded ``where:`` engine
  (``_apply_where`` + the predicate set + ``_ensure_columns`` +
  ``_ensure_boolean_predicate`` + ``_duplicate_column_names``) is one
  module; the deliberately divergent
  ``validations/reference_validations._condition_mask`` /
  ``_apply_when`` engine remains local because it carries
  ``_format_scalar`` / ``_plain_value`` normalisation and
  ``enabled_when`` switch-frame integration that the bounded engine
  does not.

== _meta Assessment

``_meta`` is now mostly an explicit data contract, not an architecture
blocker.

What changed in practice:

* The package splits did not change any ``_meta`` write or read shape.
  The inventory's namespacing (``xref_crosstable``, ``cell_codecs``,
  ``compact_multiaxis``, ``derived``, ``frame_lifecycle``,
  ``helper_policies``, ``legend_blocks``, ``sheets``,
  ``sparse_defaults``, ``split_by_discriminator``, ``workbook_view``,
  ``constraints``) is intact, and every owning module continues to
  hold its own ``_META_KEY`` constant inside its package.
* Cross-step coupling that the inventory called "implicit meta-key
  contract" (``sparse_defaults`` <-> ``xref_crosstable``;
  ``project_by_role`` reading ``xref_crosstable`` /
  ``workbook_view`` / ``sheets`` / ``derived``;
  ``derived_column_policy`` reading
  ``derived.sheets.<output>.enrich_lookup`` plus durable
  ``sheets.*.helper_columns`` plus v2 FK policy) is unchanged. The
  package splits made these contracts more visible by co-locating
  producer and consumer of each namespace inside the same package's
  files (xref dense-axes writeback, cell_codec writer, enrich_lookup
  provenance writer).
* The ``frames["_meta"]`` mutable-dict pattern is still the dominant
  cross-step coupling medium. No alternative was introduced, and no
  alternative is on the immediate horizon outside the
  ``schema_maintenance`` write-intent question.

Where ``_meta`` still acts as an architecture blocker:

* ``column_roles/resolver.py`` reads four ``_meta`` roots
  (``xref_crosstable``, ``workbook_view.sheets``,
  ``sheets.*.helper_columns``, ``derived.sheets.*.helper_columns``)
  via heuristic merge. This is the "interim" resolver; the planned
  ``_meta.derived.sheets.*.column_roles`` channel would collapse
  those four reads into one. The wave correctly did not touch it.
* ``validations/validate_columns.py:add_validations`` still mutates
  ``frames["_meta"]`` in place via ``setdefault`` (inventory
  comprehension note still applies). This is inconsistent with the
  rest of the transformations layer but is not new.
* ``schema_maintenance/meta_update.py`` still treats ``_meta`` as a
  rewrite target across all ``SUPPORTED_ROOT_NAMES``. Adding a new
  root requires updating an allowlist - a known but contained shape.

Net: ``_meta`` is no longer the thing standing in the way of clean
package boundaries; the boundaries that exist are clean despite it.
The remaining ``_meta`` complexity is concentrated in modules that the
audits already labelled out of scope.

== Risk Patterns Observed

The waves did not surface new risk patterns. What they did surface is a
clearer picture of the risk patterns that were already present and now
are easier to reason about because the surrounding noise is gone:

* **Frozen "interim" pattern.** ``column_roles/resolver.py`` and
  ``transformations/helpers.py`` are both modules the inventory and
  the Opus review flagged as transitional. Three packages
  (``project_by_role``, ``validate_columns``, the FK helpers
  reorder path) are pinned to those interim shapes. The risk is not
  in the modules themselves but in how easy it would be to refactor
  the interim shape and silently break the dependents. A future
  cleanup should sequence: contract decision first, dependents next,
  interim removal last.
* **Two ``Finding`` dataclasses still coexist.** ``finding_frame.Finding``
  is a 7-column tabular-record serializer (rule_type, frame, columns,
  row_index, value, severity, message); ``validations/findings.Finding``
  is a category/sheet/column/detail/severity model consumed by
  ``apply_severity_policy``. The naming collision is a comprehension
  risk for new readers; the two reviews both reject collapse without
  a behaviour decision. Not a regression.
* **Deferred-execution sub-language in ``workbook_views``.** The
  ``where``/``sort_by``/``join``/``pivot`` keys parsed into
  ``_meta.workbook_view`` are applied by the rendering pipeline, not
  the domain layer. Domain-only audits cannot see how completely the
  renderer honours those keys. Risk pattern is "split-brain
  contract": writer and consumer in different layers.
* **Reserved future contracts.** ``dense_axes`` in
  ``compact_multiaxis`` still raises ``NotImplementedError`` for any
  non-``None`` argument; the
  ``compact_multiaxis_package_review.adoc`` DEFER explicitly cites
  this. Risk is callers configuring it and getting a runtime error.
* **Schema-maintenance ``WriteIntent`` not enforced.** The enum is
  declared but ``apply_schema_maintenance`` applies changes regardless
  of ``DRY_RUN``. This is documented in the candidates list (C008)
  and remains a behaviour-clarification question, not a bug.

The waves themselves introduced no new risk patterns. No new public
API; no new ``_meta`` shape; no new cross-cluster edge; no new import
cycle. The ``primitives.py`` deviation in ``xref_crosstable/`` is
documented and load-time clean.

== Human Readability

Net positive on every metric the waves targeted, with a small mixed
signal on operability.

For human readers:

* Three readable narratives where there used to be three large flat
  files. ``operation.py`` in each new package reads as the public
  step; the supporting submodule reads as one focused concern.
* Function ordering in the moved bodies preserves the original source
  order (the ``domain_cleanup_wave_review`` notes the one
  unintentional body change was caught and reverted).
* The error texts in the consolidated legend reader are byte-identical
  to the originals for the four shared cases, with a single
  consciously-strict tightening for the compact-multiaxis NaN-token
  edge - a tightening that the review documented and that the test
  suite cannot detect.
* The new private utilities are short, single-concern, and explicitly
  scoped via module docstrings (e.g. ``_cell_primitives.py`` explicitly
  states what is in scope and what is intentionally left local).
* Module docstrings on the new package submodules name the concern
  ("dense-axis reconstruction"; "scalar half of the cell_codec
  package"; "policy resolution helpers") without overselling.

For AI-only operability:

* The new layout has more files - eight new modules across the three
  packages, plus the three shared utilities. Each file is small and
  searchable, but a tool that previously located all xref logic by
  ``grep`` on one path now has to consider three paths inside
  ``transformations/xref_crosstable/``.
* This is a worthwhile trade for human readers and a mild slowdown for
  agentic search. Net assessment: the waves favour human readability
  slightly more than AI operability, which matches the stated wave
  intent. No agentic workflow is broken; the registry strings, test
  imports, and ``__init__.py`` facades preserve every previously-stable
  entry point.

== Next Cleanup Frontiers

Ranked from highest-value / lowest-risk to lower-value or more
strategic. Each entry is a candidate, not a proposed implementation.

. **Resolve the ``column_roles`` interim contract.** The biggest
  structural unknown remaining. ``column_roles/resolver.py`` reads
  four ``_meta`` roots via heuristic merge; the planned
  ``_meta.derived.sheets.*.column_roles`` channel would make the
  resolver thin. Three downstream modules
  (``project_by_role``, ``validate_columns``, FK helpers reorder)
  are pinned to today's shape. This is the highest-leverage cleanup
  available: it unblocks subsequent splits, removes a flagged
  comprehension hazard, and reduces ``_meta`` read shapes in
  three modules. Risk: medium - requires a contract decision before
  any code moves. Sequencing rule: decide contract, migrate
  dependents, retire interim.
. **Split ``transformations/helpers.py`` (Sonnet C003).** ~318 LOC
  mixing FK-aware reordering (``reorder_helpers_next_to_fk``, which
  imports ``fk_helpers/policy``) with header/prefix utilities
  (``mark_helpers``, ``clean_aux_columns``, ``flatten_headers``,
  ``unflatten_headers``) that have nothing to do with FKs. Two
  registry strings pin this path
  (``pipeline/registry.py`` lines 34, 38, 42), so a move is not
  zero-cost. After C003 the FK-aware bit naturally lands in
  ``fk_helpers/reorder.py`` and the rest becomes a small
  ``column_utils.py``. Risk: low-medium - mechanical move plus three
  registry target-string updates.
. **FK helpers cluster review.** The deferred cluster
  (``transformations/fk_helpers/``, ``validations/fk_helpers.py``,
  ``fk_relations.py``, ``helper_policies.py``, plus ``policy.py`` as
  the cluster contract surface) is the largest concentration of
  ``_meta`` read shapes still untouched. The Sonnet C006
  (``validate_fk_helpers`` raise-vs-return) clarification slots
  naturally here. Risk: medium - high blast radius across four
  packages.
. **``schema_maintenance`` WriteIntent semantics.** Decide whether
  ``DRY_RUN`` is enforced internally or left as a caller contract,
  document the decision, then optionally split ``meta_update.py``
  per-meta-root after the semantics question is resolved. Risk:
  medium - behaviour-clarification, not pure refactor.
. **``reference_validations`` predicate-engine review.** Decide
  whether the local ``_condition_mask``/``_apply_when``/
  ``_plain_value``/``_format_scalar`` predicate engine should grow
  into a separate shared module that the bounded ``_where_predicates``
  could extend with ``optional_missing`` and switch-frame
  integration, or stay deliberately local. Until that decision is
  made, splitting ``reference_validations.py`` would freeze the
  wrong seam. Risk: medium.
. **Two ``Finding`` dataclasses naming clarity (Sonnet C004
  narrowed).** Either rename one of them or document the
  coexistence in code where it bites readers most
  (``validations/fk_helpers.py:FKFinding = Finding`` aliasing).
  Pure naming change. Risk: low.
. **``workbook_views`` deferred-execution audit (cross-layer).**
  The transformation sub-language stored in ``_meta.workbook_view``
  needs the renderer side reviewed in tandem. Out of scope for a
  domain-only audit, but on the horizon.
. **Dead ``prefix`` parameter (Sonnet C007).** Add deprecation
  warning or docstring fix on
  ``drop_helpers(prefix=...)`` and
  ``reorder_helpers_next_to_fk(helper_prefix=...)``. Trivial; could
  ride along with the helpers.py split.
. **``compact_multiaxis`` package formation (Sonnet C005-aware).**
  Per its own review, defer until ``dense_axes`` is implemented or
  some other concrete pressure appears. After the legend-reader
  consolidation, the group-column extension is even thinner, which
  weakens the case further. No action.

== Recommendation

Pause

The wave delivered every behaviour-preserving slice the package-
boundary review identified as safe-first. Each subsequent frontier
either requires a contract decision (``column_roles`` interim,
``schema_maintenance`` ``WriteIntent``, predicate engine), touches
the deferred FK cluster (a separate cluster review), or is small
enough to bundle with one of those when its time comes
(``helpers.py`` split, dead ``prefix`` parameter, two-``Finding``
naming).

Continuing immediately would mean either:

* attacking ``reference_validations`` or ``schema_maintenance`` without
  the contract decisions those modules need, which risks freezing the
  wrong seam;
* attacking the FK cluster without a cluster-scope review, which the
  Opus boundary review explicitly defers;
* or doing a smaller-gain slice (e.g. compact_multiaxis package) that
  the dedicated review already recommended deferring.

The recommended next move is a short pause to take a contract
decision - the ``column_roles`` interim is the highest-leverage
unknown - and then resume with a slice that decision unlocks.
Switching concern is not warranted: domain layout, ``_meta``
discipline, and the test baseline are all in a good state, and the
remaining domain frontiers are sequential, not blocking.

== Final Summary

After the cleanup and package-formation waves, the domain layer reads
as a small, consistent set of single-concern modules and three
small packages. Verbatim duplication of cell primitives, the bounded
``where`` engine, duplicate-column detection, and the legend-block
schema walk is gone. Two large flat modules and one mid-sized flat
module became packages with minimal facade re-exports; the
package-formation pattern is now a documented template visible in the
code. Three private shared utilities live where the inventory
recommended them. No public API, ``_meta`` key, or registry string
moved; tests, baseline, and roundtrip coverage are unchanged. The
remaining dense areas (``reference_validations.py``,
``schema_maintenance/meta_update.py``, the FK cluster,
``workbook_views.py``, ``derived_column_policy.py``) are exactly the
modules the prior reviews placed in the deferred bucket, and they
remain there for the same reasons. The right next move is a brief
pause to take one contract decision - most usefully the
``column_roles`` interim - before opening another structural slice.
